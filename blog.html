CREATIVE EL DIARIO: An Analysis of Creative Writing & Artwork featured in 'El Diario de la Gente"

Equally Created By: Shiva Darian, Jenna Gersie, Xueyue Liu, & Wendy Norris

February, 2020

El Diario de la Gente was an independent newspaper primarily published for and by Chicanx students at the University of Colorado Boulder between 1972 and 1983. Through 62 issues, the newspaper represents the extraordinary yet complicated history of the Chicanx Rights Movement in Boulder, the state of Colorado, and the world. The authors and editors covered a wide range of topics, including boycotts and protests, the tragic events surrounding Los Seis de Boulder, and the friction between the Chicanx student community and the CU Boulder administration. The activist newspaper also served as a creative platform for poetry, art, and literature.

Creative El Diario is a portion of a broader project, Digital El Diario, which aims to highlight that history through five interdisciplinary readings of the newspaper that employ Digital Humanities methods, techniques, and values.

Creative El Diario is a project is centered on archival justice and historical recovery of the Chicanx student movement at the University of Colorado Boulder. Using El Diario de la Gente, we created a plain-text corpus and applied computational and digital research methods in order to explore this under-researched yet significant history.

THE 2017 KERMANSHAH EARTHQUAKE: An Information System Design For Improving Disaster Relief

Shiva Darian & Saeid Tizpaz Niari

April 2019

After analyzing the aftermath of November 2017 Kermanshah Earthquake, the major problems that emerged surrounded misinformation and disorganization. Specifically, organizations were not coordinating with one another to design a relief plan that considered other entities. This led to an over abundance of supplies like food being brought to the disaster site, while neglecting other essential supplies, like tents and blankets. Many organizations effectively wasted resources on food, while residents were forced to face exposure to the winter weather.

This system design creates a space for collaboration and planning between the private, local, humanitarian relief organizations in order to more efficiently share information and plan relief efforts. The design also includes an algorithmic approach to decide what supplies each organization should bring based off of various constraints. The algorithm prioritizes the actual, real-time needs of residents, while also considering each organization's interest of maximizing the number of supplies donated. The algorithm is based on optimization techniques, and includes linear constraints.
